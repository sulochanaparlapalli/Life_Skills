## 1. What is the Feynman Technique?

The Feynman Technique is a learning method where you understand a topic deeply by explaining it in very simple words as if teaching a beginner.

## 2. What was the most interesting story or idea for you?

The most interesting idea is that learning is not just about studying harder, but about using two modes of thinking—focused and diffused thinking together, and how switching between them helps solve difficult problems and generate creative ideas (like insights coming during relaxation or breaks).

## 3. What are active and diffused modes of thinking?

* Active (Focused) mode:
A concentrated way of thinking where you focus deeply on one problem and solve it step-by-step (used in coding, solving equations, etc.).
* Diffused mode:
A relaxed thinking state where the brain works in the background, helping you make connections and get creative ideas (happens during walking, resting, or sleeping).

## 4. What are the steps to take when approaching a new topic? Only mention the points.

* Deconstruct the skill
* Learn enough to self-correct
* Remove barriers to practice
* Practice for at least 20 hours

## 5. What are some of the actions you can take going forward to improve your learning process?

* Focus on 100% involvement while learning or coding
* Avoid distractions (silent phone, no social media during work time)
* Practice deep work regularly
* Track and manage time using productivity tools
* Learn concepts by asking: where it is used, what it means, and how to code it
* Break topics into small parts for easier understanding
* Practice consistently instead of just reading theory
* Teach or explain concepts in your own words
* Communicate early when facing difficulties or doubts
* Use deadlines properly to plan and complete work on time
