## 2. Tiny Habits by BJ Fogg - Core Message

### Question 2: How can you use B = MAP to make making new habits easier? What are M, A and P.

B = MAP means that a behavior happens when three things come together:

- **M (Motivation):** The desire to do the behavior.
- **A (Ability):** How easy the behavior is to do.
- **P (Prompt):** A reminder or trigger to perform the behavior.

To build a new habit, make the behavior simple, connect it to an existing routine, and use a clear prompt.

### Question 3: Why it is important to "Shine" or Celebrate after each successful completion of habit? (This is the most important concept in today's topic. Whatever you celebrate becomes a habit)

Celebrating immediately after completing a habit creates a positive feeling in the brain. This positive emotion strengthens the behavior and makes it more likely to become a regular habit.

## 4. Book Summary of Atomic Habits

### Question 5: What is the book's perspective about Identity?

The book explains that lasting habits are built by focusing on identity. Instead of only setting goals, you should become the type of person who naturally performs the habit, such as thinking, "I am a disciplined software developer."

### Question 6: Write about the book's perspective on how to make a habit easier to do?

The book suggests reducing friction and starting with very small actions. Prepare your environment, lower the effort required, and begin with a simple version of the habit so that it is easy to repeat consistently.

### Question 7: Write about the book's perspective on how to make a habit harder to do?

To reduce unwanted habits, increase friction by removing triggers and adding obstacles. When a behavior becomes inconvenient, you are less likely to repeat it.
