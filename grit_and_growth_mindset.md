# Grit and Growth Mindset

## 1. Grit

### Question 1: Paraphrase (summarize) the video in a few (1 or 2) lines. Use your own words.

Grit means working hard for a long time on your goals without giving up, even when things are difficult. Success comes from hard work and consistency, not just talent.

---

## 2. Introduction to Growth Mindset

### Question 2: Paraphrase (summarize) the video in a few (1 or 2) lines in your own words.

A growth mindset is the belief that skills and intelligence can be developed through learning, practice, and effort. Challenges and failures are part of the learning process.

---

## 3. Understanding Internal Locus of Control

### Question 3: What is the Internal Locus of Control? What is the key point in the video?

Internal locus of control means you believe your success or failure depends on your own actions, not luck or other people.

**Key point:** When you take responsibility, you stay motivated and improve faster.

---

## 4. How to build a Growth Mindset

### Question 4: What are the key points mentioned by speaker to build growth mindset (explanation not needed).

- Embrace challenges
- Learn from feedback and mistakes
- Focus on continuous improvement
- Value effort over ability
- Develop persistence
- Stay open to learning
- Treat failure as feedback

---

## 5. Mindset - A MountBlue Warrior Reference Manual

### Question 5: What are your ideas to take action and build Growth Mindset?

- Take responsibility for my learning and progress
- Practice consistently and improve daily
- Actively seek feedback and apply it
- Break complex problems into smaller tasks
